Personal Car Project – Motorbike Engine Swap and Custom Gearbox

This is the longest and most challenging project I have taken on to date. Since mid-2025 I have been swapping a large motorcycle engine into a small car. The project has required solving many engineering challenges including packaging, reverse gear solutions, wiring integration, gearing, and CAN bus communication.

This image shows the car's wiring harness which I had to carefully dissect and reuse. Accessories such as lights and windscreen wipers cannot be controlled by the motorbike electrical system, so I had to study both vehicles’ wiring diagrams and determine how to integrate them.

Another milestone in the project was securing a sponsorship from JLCCNC. In exchange for promotional videos, I was able to manufacture custom CNC parts at a reduced cost. One of the first components was an aluminium flange designed to mount the vehicle’s differential bearing.

I also designed a custom sprocket shaft to achieve a desirable final drive ratio, balancing top speed and low-end torque for the car.

Electronics and Mechanical Projects

A variable gain microphone amplifier built on a breadboard. The circuit features a multi-stage Chebyshev filter and adjustable gain controlled by a potentiometer.

A DC motor designed and built with a group of fellow students. The project involved constructing the commutator, winding the coils, and ensuring proper electrical continuity and mechanical alignment.

A custom bed frame designed and built from scratch. This project balanced cost, functionality, and convenience, resulting in a design that was cheaper than commercial options while meeting my exact requirements.

Assisting a friend with a full motorbike engine replacement. The process required dismantling much of the bike and understanding the interaction between the electrical and mechanical subsystems.
