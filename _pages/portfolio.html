Research and Team Projects

For my Industry thesis at Elexon I've been developing a calibration jig to calibrate magnetometers with a helmholtz coil. This board uses a pi pico to retrieve test data from a 3 different tri-axial magnetometers. I've also had to develop a jig to mount the DUT magnetometers and the control magnetometer used by the helmholtz coil to ensure they are aligned for optimal calibration.

During my Vacation Research Experience Scheme (VRES) project my supervisor gave me unlimited scope for a topic which wasn't well defined, I took it upon myself to learn CAD in fusion360 so that I could properly generate a design for the device I'd envisioned creating.

Here is the final outcome of the VRES project, this was an extremely cheap gravity powered heliostat cleaning device made from laser cut acrylic, 3d printed parts, and other common materials. provided more resources or time a more advanced prototype could've been made with laser cut parts and cheap gears.

As a part of VRES we gave a speech at the end of our project to the other students in the program. You can actually see my profile image to the left is where I won the peoples choice award for best speech.

Personal Car Project – Motorbike Engine Swap and Custom Gearbox

This is the longest and most challenging project I have taken on to date. Since mid-2025 I have been swapping a large motorcycle engine into a small car. The project has required solving many engineering challenges including packaging, reverse gear solutions, wiring integration, gearing, and CAN bus communication.

This image shows the car's wiring harness which I had to carefully dissect and reuse. Accessories such as lights and windscreen wipers cannot be controlled by the motorbike electrical system, so I had to study both vehicles’ wiring diagrams and determine how to integrate them.

Another milestone in the project was securing a sponsorship from JLCCNC. In exchange for promotional videos, I was able to manufacture custom CNC parts at a significantly reduced cost. One of the first components was an aluminium flange designed to mount the vehicle’s differential bearings.

I also designed a custom sprocket shaft to achieve a desirable final drive ratio, balancing top speed and low-end torque for the car. This is part of a double chain reduction gear box, which has its own challenges (i.e. how do you tension two perpendicular chains independently?)

Electronics and Mechanical Projects

A variable gain two stage filter paired with a microphone built on a breadboard. The circuit features a multi-stage Chebyshev filter with an adjustable gain controlled by a potentiometer.

A DC motor designed and built for our electromagnetics and machines group assignment. The project involved constructing the commutator, winding the coils, and ensuring proper electrical continuity and mechanical alignment.

A custom bed frame I designed and built from scratch. One of the main constraints of this project was how to get it from the garage to my bedroom, I overcame this by designing the legs to be removeable whilst still being sufficiently rigid. This project balanced cost, functionality, and convenience, resulting in a design that was cheaper than commercial options while meeting my exact requirements.

Assisting a friend with a full motorbike engine replacement. The process required dismantling much of the bike and understanding the interaction between the electrical and mechanical subsystems.
